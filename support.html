FretShed is a guitar practice app that generates unique solos for ear training. No tabs. Just you, your guitar, and your ear. Designed for intermediate to advanced players who want to sharpen their listening skills and break out of the patterns they already know.

Frequently Asked Questions

What genres does FretShed support?

Rock, blues, jazz, metal, and funk. Each genre has its own vocabulary. The phrasing, the note choices, the feel. More genres are coming.

How does looping work?

Flip the loop switch in the player to turn it on. Then drag the left and right markers to bracket the phrase you want to work on. FretShed loops that section on repeat so you can sit with it until your ear locks in. Flip the switch again when you are done.

Can I slow down a solo without changing the pitch?

Yes. Drop the playback speed to as low as 50% and the pitch stays exactly where it is. Work a phrase slowly until your hands know it, then push the speed up until it's at tempo.

How do I cancel my subscription?

Subscriptions are managed through your Apple ID. Go to Settings → [your name] → Subscriptions on your iPhone or iPad, select FretShed, and cancel from there. You keep access until the end of the current billing period.

How do I request a refund?

Refunds go through Apple. Head to reportaproblem.apple.com, find your FretShed purchase, and submit a request from there.

Are the solos truly unique each time?

Every solo is generated fresh. You will not hear the same thing twice. That is the point. Your ear needs unfamiliar material to grow. Familiar material just confirms what you already know.

Is FretShed available on Mac?

iPhone and iPad now. Mac is coming.

Does FretShed require an internet connection?

Yes, to generate a solo. The processing happens on our servers. Once it is downloaded to your device, you can practice with it offline.
